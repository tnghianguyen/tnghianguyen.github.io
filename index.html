About me

Hello! I am from Vietnam and my Vietnamese name is Nguyễn Trung Nghĩa. To be consistent with my English legal documents, I prefer to use Trung Nghia as my first name and Nguyen as my last name. I am currently a first year PhD student in Mathematics at Rutgers University-New Brunswick.

Research

Talks/Presentations

  • In August 2021, I delivered an online talk on brief introduction to tensor categories in the language of string diagrams in the Workshop "Tannakian Categories" hosted by Professor Phung Ho Hai and sponsored by Hanoi Institute of Mathematics.
  • In September 2020 at the REU Symposium, I gave a talk on basic theory of affine group schemes.

Teaching and Outreach

  • In Fall 2021, I was a teaching assistant to MTH00086 Discrete Mathematics at VNUHCM-US.
  • In Spring 2021, I was one of the two instructors of the selected team of VNUHCM-US to enter Vietnam Mathematical Olympiad for Undergraduates, giving lectures on problem-solving strategies in linear and abstract algebra.
  • Apart from that, I have been an enthusiastic long-standing member of Projects in Mathematics and Applications (PiMA) where I have been serving as multiple pivotal roles such as an organizer, a counselor and a mentor.
